# Camera Mount Design Notes

## Purpose

This mount is intended to position the IR filter directly in front of the camera
while supporting a clean and practical mechanical assembly for the tracking
setup.

## Design Goals

The mount was designed to:

- hold the IR filter securely in front of the camera lens
- minimize visible light leakage around the filter and camera opening
- keep the optical path as unobstructed as possible
- remain easy to assemble, remove, and re-fit during testing
- provide a stable structure for repeatable camera positioning

## Optical Intent

The main optical purpose of the mount is to support IR-based sensing by placing
the filter in a controlled position ahead of the camera. A close-fitting
mechanical enclosure helps reduce stray visible light entering from the sides,
which improves the usefulness of the filter in mixed lighting conditions.

## Mechanical Intent

The mount is intended to be simple rather than highly complex. It should:

- support the filter without excessive movement
- allow straightforward access during maintenance or part replacement
- be practical to print and handle as a prototype part
- avoid making camera installation unnecessarily difficult

## Assembly Considerations

During assembly, the mount should make it easy to:

- place the filter in the correct position
- align the filter and camera opening
- remove the filter or mount when adjustments are needed
- reassemble the unit without damaging the printed part

## Prototype Notes

This design prioritize rapid prototyping and iterative testing over
full enclosure sealing. The key balance is between optical shielding and ease of
use: the mount should block as much unwanted visible light as possible while
still being convenient to assemble and disassemble.
